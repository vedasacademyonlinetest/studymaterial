Part 1 — 1D Motion
Fundamental Quantities
Scalars vs Vectors
  • DistanceTotal path length (scalar, always ≥ 0)
  • DisplacementShortest path from start → end (vector, can be –ve)
  • SpeedDistance / Time (scalar, always ≥ 0)
  • VelocityDisplacement / Time (vector)
  • AccelerationΔv / Δt (vector, + or –)
Average vs Instantaneous
  • Avg SpeedTotal distance / Total time
  • Avg VelocityNet displacement / Total time
  • Inst. Velocityv = dx/dt (slope of x–t graph)
  • Inst. Accel.a = dv/dt (slope of v–t graph)
  • Displacement= Area under v–t graph
Equations of Motion (Uniform Acceleration)
Velocity–Time
v = u + at
Displacement–Time
s = ut + ½at²
Velocity–Displacement
v² = u² + 2as
Average Velocity
vavg = (u + v) / 2
nth Second Rule
sn = u + a(2n–1)/2
Stopping Distance
d = u² / 2a
⚡ Olympiad Tip — nth Second Rule
Displacement in nth second: sn = u + a(2n–1)/2. This is displacement IN the nth second, not after n seconds. Ratio of distances in 1st, 2nd, 3rd second (from rest) = 1 : 3 : 5 : … (odd numbers).
Motion Graphs — Key Interpretations
x – t Graph
  • Slope= Instantaneous velocity
  • Straight lineUniform velocity (a = 0)
  • ParabolaUniform acceleration
  • Slope = 0Body at rest
  • Negative slopeMoving backward
  • Inflection ptAcceleration changes sign
v – t Graph
  • Slope= Instantaneous acceleration
  • Area (above x-axis)= + displacement
  • Area (below x-axis)= – displacement
  • v = 0Body momentarily at rest
  • Curve concave upAcceleration increasing
  • Horizontal lineConstant velocity
Free Fall & Vertical Motion
Key Results (take g = 10 m/s² downward)
Time to reach max height
t = u / g
Maximum height
H = u² / 2g
Time of flight (up+down)
T = 2u / g
Velocity at ground (drop h)
v = √(2gh)
⚠ Common Error
Speed of projection = speed of landing (same height). BUT time of ascent = time of descent only when air resistance is zero. In air resistance problems, ascent time < descent time.
Olympiad Problems — 1D Motion
★ Olympiad 1
A ball is dropped from the top of a tower. In the last second before hitting the ground it covers 9/25 of the total height. Find the height of the tower. (g = 10 m/s²)
1
Let total time = T seconds. Total height: H = ½gT²
2
Distance in first (T–1) seconds: h₁ = ½g(T–1)²
3
Distance in last second: H – h₁ = ½g[T² – (T–1)²] = ½g(2T – 1)
4
Given condition: ½g(2T–1) = (9/25)·½gT²
→ 25(2T–1) = 9T² → 9T² – 50T + 25 = 0
5
Solving: T = 5 s or T = 5/9 s (rejected, since 5/9 < 1)
✓ H = ½ × 10 × 25 = 125 m
Key InsightForm a quadratic in T using the nth-second formula — always reject unphysical roots.
★ Olympiad 2
A train accelerates from rest to v m/s covering distance d₁, then decelerates to rest covering d₂. If the total time is T, find maximum speed v in terms of d₁, d₂, T only.
1
Time for acceleration phase: t₁ = 2d₁/v. Time for deceleration: t₂ = 2d₂/v
2
T = t₁ + t₂ = 2d₁/v + 2d₂/v = 2(d₁+d₂)/v
✓ v = 2(d₁ + d₂) / T
Key InsightFor any uniformly accelerated/decelerated segment: time = 2 × (distance/peak speed). Elegant elimination of a₁ and a₂!
★ Olympiad 3
Two balls are thrown vertically upward from the same point with speeds u₁ and u₂ (u₁ > u₂). Find the time after which their separation is maximum, and find that separation.
1
Position of ball 1: x₁ = u₁t – ½gt². Ball 2: x₂ = u₂t – ½gt²
2
Separation: Δx = x₁ – x₂ = (u₁–u₂)t. This is linear — separation increases until ball 2 falls back.
3
Ball 2 hits ground at T₂ = 2u₂/g. After this, ball 2 is on ground, ball 1 still in air if T₁ = 2u₁/g > T₂.
4
Maximum separation occurs at t = T₂ = 2u₂/g:
Δxmax = (u₁–u₂)·(2u₂/g) = 2u₂(u₁–u₂)/g
✓ t = 2u₂/g, Separation = 2u₂(u₁ – u₂)/g
Key InsightBoth balls experience same g, so relative acceleration = 0. Separation grows linearly until one ball hits the ground.
Part 2 — 2D Motion
Vector Decomposition & Relative Motion
Component Method
  • x-componentAₓ = A cosθ
  • y-componentAᵧ = A sinθ
  • MagnitudeA = √(Aₓ² + Aᵧ²)
  • Directionθ = tan⁻¹(Aᵧ/Aₓ)
  • Principlex & y motions are independent
Relative Motion
  • vAB= vA – vB (A relative to B)
  • River-boatvactual = vboat + vriver
  • Min driftAim upstream at sin⁻¹(vr/vb)
  • Min timeAim perpendicular to river
  • Rain-manvrain-man = vrain – vman
Projectile Motion
Standard Launch (angle θ, speed u, from ground)
x-velocity (constant)
vₓ = u cosθ
y-velocity
vᵧ = u sinθ – gt
Time of Flight
T = 2u sinθ / g
Max Height
H = u² sin²θ / 2g
Range
R = u² sin2θ / g
Max Range (θ = 45°)
Rmax = u² / g
Trajectory (parabola)
y = x tanθ – gx²/(2u²cos²θ)
Speed at any point
v = √(vₓ² + vᵧ²)
⚡ Key Properties
Complementary angles (e.g. 30° & 60°) give same range. • At highest point: vₓ = u cosθ, vᵧ = 0. • Horizontal velocity is always constant. • Range is same for θ and (90°–θ). • At any height h: v² = u² – 2gh (only y-component affected by g).
Uniform Circular Motion
Angular velocity
ω = 2π/T = 2πf
Speed
v = rω
Centripetal Acc.
ac = v²/r = ω²r
Direction
Always → center
⚠ Crucial Distinction
In UCM: speed is constant but velocity changes (direction changes). Therefore acceleration is non-zero even though |v| = const. The acceleration is centripetal, NOT tangential.
Olympiad Problems — 2D Motion
★ Olympiad 4
A projectile is fired at 45°. At the highest point, its speed is 10√2 m/s. Another projectile is fired with same speed but at 30°. Find the ratio of their ranges. (g = 10 m/s²)
1
At highest point, only horizontal component remains: vtop = u cos45° = u/√2
2
10√2 = u/√2 → u = 20 m/s
3
R₁ (at 45°) = u²/g = 400/10 = 40 m
R₂ (at 30°) = u² sin60°/g = 400·(√3/2)/10 = 20√3 m
✓ R₁ : R₂ = 40 : 20√3 = 2 : √3
Key InsightSpeed at highest point directly gives u cosθ — always extract launch speed from this before computing range.
★ Olympiad 5
A river 100 m wide flows at 3 m/s. A boat can do 5 m/s in still water. (a) Find minimum time to cross. (b) Find minimum drift. (c) Find angle for minimum drift.
1
(a) Min time: Aim perpendicular. t = d/vboat = 100/5 = 20 s. Drift = 3×20 = 60 m.
2
(b) Min drift: Aim upstream at angle α where sinα = vr/vb = 3/5 → α = 37° upstream.
3
Effective crossing speed = √(vb² – vr²) = √(25–9) = 4 m/s. Time = 100/4 = 25 s. Drift = 0.
✓ Min time = 20 s | Min drift = 0 (aim at 37° upstream) | Crossing speed for zero drift = 4 m/s
Key InsightZero drift is possible only if vboat > vriver. If vboat < vriver, drift cannot be zero, only minimized.
★ Olympiad 6
A ball is thrown horizontally from height H with speed u. Another ball is dropped from the same height. Show that the horizontal ball always has greater speed, and find their speed ratio when both hit the ground.
1
Both take same time T = √(2H/g) to fall (same vertical drop, same initial vertical velocity = 0).
2
Vertical speed at ground for both: vᵧ = gT = √(2gH).
3
Dropped ball speed: v₂ = vᵧ = √(2gH).
Horizontal ball: v₁ = √(u² + 2gH).
✓ v₁/v₂ = √(u² + 2gH) / √(2gH) > 1 always
Key InsightSame fall time, same vertical speed — the horizontal ball only adds to its speed. Classic independence-of-motions demonstration.
★★ Olympiad 7 — Tricky
A stone is projected at angle θ above horizontal. It has speed v₁ at height h and speed v₂ at height 2h. Prove that the speed at height 3h is √(3v₂² – 2v₁²), and find θ.
1
Horizontal component is constant = v₀ (say). Vertical components:
v₁² = v₀² + vy₁², v₂² = v₀² + vy₂²
2
Using v² = u² – 2gh for vertical component only:
vy₁² = vy₀² – 2gh, vy₂² = vy₀² – 4gh
3
v₁² – v₂² = 2gh → h = (v₁² – v₂²)/2g. At height 3h: vy₃² = vy₀² – 6gh.
4
v₃² = v₀² + vy₃² = v₀² + vy₂² – 2gh = v₂² – (v₁²–v₂²) = 3v₂² – 2v₁²
✓ v₃ = √(3v₂² – 2v₁²) [QED]
Key InsightNever use resultant speed directly in energy equations — always separate horizontal (constant) and vertical (changing) components. The horizontal component is the "hidden constant" in all projectile problems.
Quick Reference — Must-Remember Facts
Olympiad Fast-Track Memory Sheet
  • Distances from restRatio in successive seconds = 1 : 3 : 5 : 7 : … (odd number series)
  • Displacement ratiosAfter 1s, 2s, 3s from rest = 1 : 4 : 9 : … (square series)
  • Same range anglesθ and (90° – θ) always give same range
  • Projectile at topKE = ½m(u cosθ)², PE is maximum, net speed is minimum
  • Velocity directionAlways tangent to trajectory path at every point
  • Relative motionIf relative acceleration = 0, bodies approach/recede linearly
  • UCM: Δv magnitude|Δv| = 2v sin(Δθ/2) ≈ vΔθ for small angles
  • Stopping ratioIf braking force same, stopping distances ∝ v² (double speed → 4× distance)
  • Avg velocity (const. a)= (u+v)/2 = velocity at t = T/2 (midtime, not midpoint)
