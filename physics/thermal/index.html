Converting Thermal Energy into Mechanical Energy

Using a Whistle Cooker as a real-life thermodynamic model | A working analogy for Steam Engines & Power Plants

Q = W + ΔU
First Law of Thermodynamics

How It Works — Visual Diagram

❓ How Does It Work?
1
♨️
Steam Generation
Water in the cooker is heated until it boils and converts to steam. As steam pressure builds, the whistle valve is pushed open and steam escapes.
Q = mcΔT
2
⚙️
Drum Rotation
The escaping steam strikes the drum and causes it to spin. The drum acts like a turbine — thermal energy converts into mechanical rotational energy.
KErot = ½Iω²
3
🪢
Rope Winding
As the drum rotates, a rope winds around it. The linear mechanical energy is transferred through the rope via the fixed overhead pulley.
W = F · d
4
🏋️
Weight Lifted — PE Gained
The rope pulls the weight upward, converting mechanical energy into gravitational potential energy. Thermal → Mechanical energy conversion is complete.
PE = mgh

⚖️
Conservation of Energy
1st Law of Thermodynamics
Energy cannot be created or destroyed — only transformed from one form to another. Q = W + ΔU
🌡️
Entropy Increases
2nd Law of Thermodynamics
No conversion is 100% efficient. Some energy always dissipates as waste heat (ΔS > 0). Efficiency η < 1.
🔄
Thermodynamic Cycle
Rankine / Carnot Cycle
Steam engines & power plants follow the Rankine cycle — the same principle scaled up from this simple cooker model.
💡 Summary

The whistle cooker model shows how Thermal Energy (heat from the flame) is used via steam pressure to spin a drum, wind a rope, and ultimately lift a weight — demonstrating a complete chain of Thermal → Mechanical Energy conversion in everyday life.
